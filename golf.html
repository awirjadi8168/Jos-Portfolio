Functionalizing Diamond

Project Overview

This project focused on the surface modification of diamond substrates for semiconductor applications. The primary goal was to successfully deposit thin metal films to optimize thermal management in high-performance electronic environments.

The Challenge

During the development phase, our primary wire bonding tool was unexpectedly decommissioned. This created a significant roadblock in our ability to adhere metal contacts to the diamond surface using our standard operating procedures.

The Solution & Pivot

Instead of halting the project, I pivoted to designing an alternative methodology. I developed a custom sputtering process to deposit the titanium and metal films directly onto the substrate. This required:

  • Calculating fracture mechanics and managing thermal stress during deposition.
  • Calibrating the sputtering equipment to ensure uniform film thickness.
  • Testing the adherence and conductivity of the newly bonded metals.

Outcome

The transition to a sputtering method not only allowed the project to continue on schedule but also resulted in a highly reliable adhesion process that proved resilient under thermal testing.
